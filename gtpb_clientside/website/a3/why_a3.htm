Strategic issue:Why Lean service organisations use A3 reports Report ID:1 By:GTwM Date:28th July 2010
1) Why are we looking at this problem, now? (Urgency review)
Rapid rate of change means we are needing to make an increasing number of strategic decisions quickly. Sometimes quality of decisions has suffered. Building consensus around these decisions has also become far harder.
2) Where are we now? (Situational analysis)
Decision making is largely reserved for senior managers, valuable input from across the organisation has been overlooked on occasions and the process can be fraught. Informing staff and building consensus is adhoc, and staff seem confused. We are on a journey to becoming Lean.
3) Where do we want to be? (Desired future state)
A better decision making approach. One that captures best practice for the service sector and that helps us delegate decision making authority appropriately and with confidence.
4) Why are we where we are now? (Root cause analysis)
As a small organisation our decision making was adhoc but inclusive and effective. Our recent growth however has meant this approach is no longer viable.
5) What do we need to do? (Countermeasures)
Designate a member of staff to learn about A3 reports
- Read references on a3reports.wordpress.com about Lean Thinking, decision making and A3 reports
- Share learning / train colleagues and staff
6) How will we know when it has been achieved? (Measures, metrics and description of what will suffice?)
- Widespread understanding and "buy in" to the value of Lean business philosophy and A3 decision making
- Staff training complete
7) How can we ensure changes are embedded? (SOP)
Require teams to have dedicated space for A3 report display.
Ensure line managers review reports as a core part of their daily supervisor duty.
8) Follow up actions to be taken (Lessons learned)
Test www.a3reports.co.uk to see what value it might add compared with a purely paper based approach.
